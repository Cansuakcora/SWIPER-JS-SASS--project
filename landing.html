Luciano Sposato MD, MBA

Professor, Department of
Clinical Neurological Sciences,
London Health Sciences Centre (LHSC),
Western University, Ontario

Luciano Sposato is a stroke neurologist and clinician-researcher. He is appointed as Professor of Neurology, London Health Sciences Centre (LHSC), Western University, London, Canada. He is the Head of LHSC/Western University Stroke Program and holds the Dr Kathleen and Henry Barnett Chair in Stroke Research. Dr Sposato is an Associate Scientist at the Robarts Research Institute and Scientist at Lawson Health Research Institute. Dr Sposato’s research focuses on the heart and brain connection. His research is unique in its approach since he investigates how brain damage affects the heart and vice-versa. He established the Heart & Brain Lab at Western University, where he leads a multidisciplinary and translational research programme involving epidemiological, clinical and experimental studies investigating the heart and brain connection. Among his major contributions, Dr Sposato has established the concepts of atrial fibrillation diagnosed after stroke (AFDAS) and stroke-induced heart injury (SIHI).

Dr Sposato has authored over 120 scientific publications. He also sits on the editorial boards of Neurology (journal of the American Academy of Neurology), Stroke (official journal of the American Heart Association), Journal of the American Heart Association (JAHA), and the World Stroke Academy (World Stroke Organization). Dr Sposato is the Editor for the neurocardiology section of Stroke Journal and Guest Associate Editor of JAHA.

Jose L. Zamorano MD, FESC

Head of Cardiology, University Hospital
Ramón y Cajal, Madrid

Jose L. Zamorano is Head of Cardiology at the University Hospital Ramón y Cajal, Madrid, and has directed the Cardiovascular Institute of the Hospital Clínico San Carlos, Madrid, Spain. Professor Zamorano has served as President of the Spanish National Research Evaluation Committee and Chair of the European Society of Cardiology (ESC) Global Scientific Affairs. He has also been President of the European Echocardiography Association of the ESC, Vice President of the ESC, as well as Chair of the ESC Guidelines Committee.

Professor Zamorano has a broad range of research interests including heart failure, ischaemic heart disease, cardiovascular risk factors and imaging modalities. Within these fields, he has published over 540 articles in peer-reviewed journals, over 400 abstracts in various symposia, and over 50 chapters in medical books, including the European Textbook of Cardiovascular Imaging.

Over the years, Professor Zamorano has received a number of prestigious Spanish and International awards, such as the Golden Plaque of the Public Health Authorities of Madrid and the Inge Edler Gold Medal, the silver medal of the ESC, and Research Award of the Spanish Society of Cardiology. He serves on numerous Editorial Boards, including the Spanish Journal of Cardiology, European Journal of Echocardiography, the American Society of Echocardiography and the European Heart Journal.
